Guide to Adding .stl files to Minecraft

Have you ever wanted to build something in Minecraft without building? You can.

Step 1: Create the .schematic file for importing it into Minecraft

To do this, you will use Tinkercad. 1. Sign up for Tinkercad. 2. Go onto Thingiverse and find a model. If you want a CoderDojo logo, click here. Please note that this logo is not in color. 3. Create a new project. 4. Click "Browse" and find your model you just downloaded. Then, click "Import". 5. Click "Download for Minecraft" from the menu "Design". 6. Click "Export". 7. Save the file somewhere.

Step 2: Install MCEdit

MCEdit will allow you to modify your Minecraft world in ways you usually can't. 1. Go to mcedit.net 2. Click "Download MCEdit". 3. Click "Click here to download MCEdit 1.0". 4. Select your OS. 5. Run the installer. This step will vary depending on your OS. For Linux, it is more complicated than it is for other OSes.

Step 3: Prepare a Minecraft world.

This step is simple. 1. Create a Minecraft world. 2. Quit Minecraft.

Step 4: Add the .schematic into Minecraft

This is also easy. 1. Launch MCEdit, and click "Quick Load". 2. Select your world and click "Load". 3. Press the 6 key. 4. Find your .schematic file and click "Open". 5. Drag the item to somewhere you want. 6. Press enter. 7. Press Esc. 8. Click "Quit".

Step 5: See your work!
